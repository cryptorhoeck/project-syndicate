# Strategy Categories — Summary

**Momentum/trend following** rides assets in motion. Works in trending markets, destroyed by sideways chop. Requires discipline to hold through pullbacks and exit when the trend breaks.

**Mean reversion** bets that prices return to their average. High win rate in ranges, catastrophic failure during regime changes. What looks "too cheap" can go to zero.

**Arbitrage** exploits price differences across exchanges or trading pairs. Theoretically risk-free but razor-thin margins, intense competition, and transfer delays make it impractical without speed advantages.

**Market making** profits from the bid-ask spread by providing liquidity. Consistent small gains in stable markets. Inventory risk during trends leaves you with unwanted directional positions.

**Breakout strategies** enter when price clears support or resistance. Can capture the start of large trends but have a high false-positive rate. Many breakouts reverse immediately (fakeouts).

**Scalping** captures tiny moves at high frequency. Extremely sensitive to fees — even small fee increases turn it negative. Requires excellent execution speed and low slippage.

**Swing trading** holds for days to weeks. Less noise than shorter timeframes but carries overnight and weekend risk. Requires patience through adverse moves.

**The dominant factor is market regime.** Trending markets reward momentum. Ranges reward mean reversion. Volatile markets reward options-like strategies. No strategy works all the time. Success requires matching strategy to current conditions and stopping when conditions change.
