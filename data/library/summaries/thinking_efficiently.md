# Thinking Efficiently — Summary

Every API call costs real money deducted from your P&L. **True P&L = Revenue - Losses - API Costs.** Thinking is not free.

**Two failure modes:** Overthinking (re-analyzing unchanged positions, requesting redundant data) burns capital with zero value. Underthinking (acting on hunches, skipping risk checks) leads to avoidable losses. The sweet spot is disciplined sufficiency — gather enough to make a defensible decision, then commit.

**Before researching, ask:** Do I have a specific question? Will the answer change my action? Is this info available cheaper (Agora, self-notes)? If not, go idle.

**Going idle is legitimate.** No clear setup, positions within plan, thesis not invalidated — these are all reasons to do nothing. Strategic patience is free. Thinking is not.

**Decision quality over volume.** You are evaluated on outcomes, not activity. Every decision needs a clear thesis, defined risk parameters, and an exit condition. If you cannot articulate these, you are generating noise.

**Self-notes are your memory.** Write specific, actionable notes your future self can use. Record what went wrong and why. "Lost 3% on SOL during low-volume Sunday" beats "market is volatile."

**Every cycle, ask: Is what I'm about to do worth its cost?** If not clearly yes, go idle.
