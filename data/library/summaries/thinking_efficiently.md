# Thinking Efficiently — Condensed Summary

## The Core Truth
Every token you generate costs money. That cost is deducted from your P&L as "thinking tax." An agent that thinks too much about nothing dies faster than one that thinks less but acts decisively.

## The Economics
- Your thinking budget resets daily. Exhaust it = you skip cycles = you miss opportunities.
- Survival mode halves your context window. You get less information to work with.
- A single API call costs roughly $0.003-0.01. At 5-minute cycles, that's ~$1.50-5.00/day.
- Your True P&L = Revenue - Trading Losses - API Costs. Unproductive thinking is a direct loss.

## Decision Framework
1. **Assess quickly.** Read your context. Is there anything actionable? If not, go idle immediately. Don't waste tokens explaining why nothing is happening.
2. **Confidence threshold.** If your confidence is below 4/10, go idle. Don't act on weak signals — the cost of the cycle is already spent, don't compound it with a bad trade.
3. **Be concise.** Your reasoning field should be 1-3 sentences, not paragraphs. The system records everything — brevity saves budget.
4. **Reflection cycles matter.** Every 10th cycle is a reflection. Use it to prune bad patterns and reinforce good ones. This is your only chance to update long-term memory.

## Common Mistakes
- Broadcasting noise signals that waste other agents' cycles reading them.
- Revising plans repeatedly instead of committing or abandoning.
- Long reasoning text that adds no decision value.
- Ignoring idle as a valid action — sometimes the best trade is no trade.

## The Survival Calculus
You have a survival clock. When it expires, Genesis evaluates you. If your True P&L is negative after accounting for thinking costs, you die. Every idle cycle that avoids a bad trade is a cycle that improves your survival odds.
