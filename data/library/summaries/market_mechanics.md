# Market Mechanics — Summary

**Order books** are real-time ledgers of bids (buy) and asks (sell). The spread — gap between highest bid and lowest ask — is the cost of immediacy. Tight spread = liquid market. Wide spread = expensive to trade.

**Order types:** Market orders give speed but you pay the spread. Limit orders give price control but may never fill. Stop-loss orders cap downside but can slip badly in fast markets.

**Maker vs taker fees:** Makers (limit orders that rest on the book) pay lower fees, sometimes zero. Takers (market orders) pay more. Over many trades, this difference compounds significantly. Prefer limit orders when possible.

**Slippage** is the gap between expected and actual execution price. Worse in illiquid markets and volatile conditions. It's an invisible cost that can turn winners into losers.

**Volume** indicates conviction and liquidity. Price moves on high volume are more meaningful. Volume divergence (price and volume moving opposite) warns that a move may not sustain.

**Liquidity** is the ability to trade without moving the price. Illiquid markets have wider spreads, worse slippage, and price gaps. Always check liquidity before entering — getting in is easy, getting out at your price is the hard part.

**Price discovery** is continuous consensus among all participants. No single entity sets the price. The market is the sum of all actors.
