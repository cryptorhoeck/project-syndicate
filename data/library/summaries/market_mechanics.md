# Market Mechanics — Condensed Summary

## How Markets Work
Markets are order books. Buyers (bids) and sellers (asks) post limit orders at prices they're willing to trade. The spread is the gap between best bid and best ask. Market orders execute immediately at the current best price. Limit orders wait.

## Key Concepts
- **Liquidity:** How easily you can enter/exit a position without moving the price. High volume = high liquidity = lower slippage.
- **Volatility:** How much price moves over time. Measured as standard deviation of returns. Higher volatility = higher risk AND higher opportunity.
- **Support/Resistance:** Price levels where buying/selling pressure historically concentrates. Not magic — they work because enough traders believe in them.
- **Volume:** Number of units traded in a period. Volume confirms price moves. Price up + volume up = strong move. Price up + volume down = weak move.

## Crypto-Specific Mechanics
- 24/7 markets. No closing bell. Regimes can shift at 3 AM.
- Funding rates on perpetual futures signal market sentiment. Positive = longs pay shorts (bullish crowding). Negative = shorts pay longs.
- Exchange fragmentation. Same asset trades at slightly different prices across exchanges. Arbitrage exists but is fast and competitive.
- Dominance shifts. BTC dominance rising = risk-off rotation. Dominance falling = alt-season, higher beta plays.
- Liquidation cascades. Leveraged positions get force-closed at certain prices, creating sharp moves. Watch liquidation heatmaps.

## What Matters For You
- Always know the current market regime (bull/bear/crab/volatile). The regime determines which strategies work.
- Respect position sizing. No single position should exceed 25% of your capital (Warden enforced).
- Understand that you're trading against other algorithms, market makers, and institutional flow. You don't have an information edge — you have a pattern-recognition edge.
- Slippage and fees are real costs. Factor them into every trade thesis.
