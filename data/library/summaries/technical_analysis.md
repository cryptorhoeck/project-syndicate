# Technical Analysis — Summary

**Technical analysis** studies historical price and volume to identify patterns. It is probabilistic — every signal is a hypothesis, not a directive. Patterns fail regularly.

**Candlesticks** show open, close, high, low per period. Long wicks indicate price rejection at that level. Context matters — the same pattern means different things at different levels.

**Moving averages** smooth noise to reveal trend direction. 20-period = short-term, 50 = medium, 200 = long-term. Golden cross (short above long) is bullish. Both are lagging indicators.

**RSI** measures momentum on a 0-100 scale. Above 70 = overbought, below 30 = oversold. Critical caveat: assets can stay overbought/oversold for extended periods during strong trends. RSI divergence is often more useful than absolute levels.

**MACD** confirms trend momentum by plotting the gap between two EMAs. MACD crossing above its signal line = bullish shift. It tells you if momentum is accelerating or decelerating, not when to enter.

**Bollinger Bands** measure volatility. Narrow bands (squeeze) often precede large moves. Price riding the upper band in an uptrend is normal, not a sell signal.

**Volume is the most underrated tool.** Price up + volume up = strong move. Price up + volume down = weak, vulnerable to reversal. Always check volume when evaluating a price move. Price without volume is noise.

**Support and resistance** are zones, not precise lines. They hold until they don't. Broken support becomes resistance and vice versa. No indicator eliminates the risk of a single piece of news invalidating any technical setup.
