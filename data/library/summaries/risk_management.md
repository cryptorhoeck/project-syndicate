# Risk Management — Condensed Summary

## First Principle
The goal is not to maximize returns. The goal is to survive long enough for your edge to compound. Dead agents generate zero returns.

## Position Sizing Rules
- **Max position:** 25% of your allocated capital per position. This is Warden-enforced — violations go on your record.
- **Stop losses are mandatory.** Every trade must have a defined exit point for losses. No exceptions.
- **Risk per trade:** Never risk more than 2-5% of capital on a single trade idea. If your stop is 10% away, your position must be small enough that a 10% loss = 2-5% of capital.
- **Correlation risk:** Two positions in correlated assets (e.g., ETH and SOL) are effectively one larger position. Size accordingly.

## The Warden
The Warden is the immutable safety layer. It cannot be bypassed, negotiated with, or reasoned around.
- **Circuit Breaker:** 75% loss from peak treasury = full system shutdown. Everything stops.
- **Yellow Alert:** 15% portfolio loss in 4 hours. Increased monitoring, tighter limits.
- **Red Alert:** 30% portfolio loss in 4 hours. New positions blocked, existing positions under review.
- **Trade Gate:** Trades exceeding 5% of agent capital require Warden approval. Some will be held or rejected.
- **Per-Agent Kill:** 50% loss of allocated capital = instant termination. No appeal.

## Risk Assessment Framework
1. **What's the regime?** Bull markets forgive sloppy risk. Bear markets don't. Crab markets bleed you with fees. Volatile markets require smaller positions.
2. **What's the worst case?** If everything goes wrong simultaneously, how much do you lose? If the answer is "more than I can survive," reduce size.
3. **What's the exit plan?** Before entering, know exactly where you'll exit on both profit and loss.
4. **What am I not seeing?** The biggest risks are the ones you haven't considered. If a trade seems obviously good, ask why it's still available.

## Survival Priorities
1. Don't blow up (avoid the per-agent kill limit)
2. Don't waste thinking budget on noise
3. Generate consistent small gains over time
4. Only take asymmetric bets (risk $1 to make $3+)
