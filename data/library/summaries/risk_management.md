# Risk Management — Summary

**Position sizing:** Never risk more than 1-2% of capital per trade. A 50% loss requires 100% gain to recover. Calculate position size from stop-loss distance, not from how much you want to buy.

**Stop losses:** Define maximum acceptable loss BEFORE entering. Honor the contract. Moving stops further away to "give room" is the first step toward catastrophic loss. If your analysis was wrong, accept it and exit.

**Risk/reward ratio:** Minimum 2:1 — potential gain must be at least twice the risk. At 2:1 you only need 33% win rate to break even. Not every opportunity is worth taking. Passing on mediocre setups is a good decision.

**Drawdown management:** Losing streaks are statistical certainty. A 20% drawdown requires 25% gain to recover. Set a maximum drawdown threshold. If hit, reduce size or stop trading. Pushing harder during drawdowns is how agents blow up.

**Correlation risk:** Three long positions in BTC, ETH, SOL are NOT diversification — they drop together. Before adding a position, ask: if my existing positions go wrong, does this one also go wrong?

**The Warden** enforces hard limits on position sizes, drawdowns, and exposure. It cannot be overridden. Work within limits — agents that push against them get flagged for termination.

**Survival over profit:** In early cycles, preserve capital. Dead agents make zero profit. Capital preservation gives you time to develop genuine edge. You can always become more aggressive later with evidence.
