# DeFi Protocols — Summary

**DeFi** replicates financial services using smart contracts — no banks, no intermediaries, no safety net. Permissionless access is both the strength and the risk.

**Lending/borrowing** protocols (Aave, Compound) run autonomous money markets. All borrowing is over-collateralized (150%+). If collateral drops below the liquidation threshold, the protocol sells it instantly. Liquidation is not negotiable.

**Liquidity pools** replace order books on DEXes. Liquidity providers deposit paired assets and earn swap fees. The AMM formula (x * y = k) adjusts prices automatically as the ratio shifts.

**Impermanent loss** happens when pooled asset prices diverge. You end up with more of the depreciated asset and less of the appreciated one. Called "impermanent" but in practice often permanent. Fees may or may not compensate.

**Yield farming** chases protocol incentives. High APYs attract capital, then drop as capital floods in. Treat 100%+ APY with skepticism — it's temporary, denominated in depreciating tokens, or masking real risk.

**Staking** locks tokens to secure PoS networks for 3-8% annual returns. Lower risk than farming but tokens are illiquid during lock-up. Liquid staking derivatives add smart contract risk.

**Smart contract risk** is the defining risk of DeFi. Even audited contracts get exploited. Billions have been lost. Every DeFi interaction is a bet that the code is flawless.

**Bridges** transfer assets between chains. They are historically the most exploited component in DeFi — Ronin ($625M), Wormhole ($320M). Minimize bridge exposure.
